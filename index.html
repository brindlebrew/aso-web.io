John G. Mayer

Conductor

Mr. Mayer received his Bachelor’s degree in Music Education from West Chester University where he studied trumpet with Dr. Kenneth Laudermilch. He continued his education at Temple University where he studied Choral Conducting with Alan Harler and trumpet with Seymour Rosenfeld. He received his Master in Music degree in Instrumental Conducting from the University of Delaware under the tutelage of Dr. Robert Streckfuss.

Mr. Mayer was a church music director for over 26 years, directing both the choir and the orchestra. He currently teaches General and Choral music at Thurgood Marshall Elementary School in Newark, Delaware. Each year he and his 4th and 5th grade students collaborate with music majors from the University of Delaware to stage an opera, which is staged both at Marshall School and on the University campus.

Mr. Mayer currently plays trumpet, flugelhorn, and trombone in several Oktoberfest bands. He resides in North Wilmington, Delaware.

History of the Orchestra

The orchestra was founded by the Audubon YMCA in 1991 as the Community Orchestra of the YMCA. This was later changed to the Audubon YMCA Symphony Orchestra. In mid-2000, the orchestra changed to its present name and was adopted by a new sponsor, namely the Montgomery County Cultural Center (MCCC) located in downtown Norristown. The valuable support of MCCC carried ASO through to the Fall of 2006 when the orchestra formed its own identity retaining the name Audubon Symphony Orchestra. As its own corporation, the mission and goals remain unchanged.

Join Us

The Audubon Symphony Orchestra is a community organization, and we’d love to invite everyone in the community who’s willing and able to play to join us. However, the reality of orchestras is that there are a limited number of seats available for those who play certain instruments.

We are always looking for new string players:

  • Violin
  • Viola
  • Cello
  • String Bass

Unfortunately, some of our wind-instrument sections are currently full, but we do need a few players who play the following instruments:

  • Clarinet
  • Oboe
  • Bassoon
  • Trombone
  • French Horn
  • Percussion

If you play a string instrument or any of the wind instruments listed above, and you’d like to join us, please send an email to http://audubonsymphony.org/contact . Send us your name, contact information and a bit of information about yourself and we will get back to you as soon as possible. Our music is advanced high-school and college-level difficulty, and we rehearse from September through April, with performances in December and May. Also, those who play all instruments are welcome to check with us from time to time to see if seats for your instrument have opened up.

We look forward to hearing from you!
